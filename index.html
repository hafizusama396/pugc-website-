About Punjab University

Punjab University Gujranwala Campus is a sub-campus of the University of the Punjab, located in Gujranwala, Punjab, Pakistan. Established in 2006, the campus offers a range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs in various disciplines, including business administration, commerce, economics, education, and information technology. The campus has state-of-the-art facilities, including air-conditioned classrooms and laboratories , a library with a collection of books, journals, and online resources, computer labs with internet facilities, sports facilities, and cafeteria and canteen facilities. The campus encourages research and extracurricular activities, including seminars, workshops, and cultural events, with a strong focus on community service and outreach programs, and has partnerships with local industries and organizations.

Success Stories

Punjab University has a rich history of producing successful alumni and achieving numerous milestones. One of the success stories is that of BFUHS, which has pipped Panjab University in civil services success rate. The university has organized interactive sessions on ' Success Stories Through UCP Journey' of Alumni of Psychology department, where alumni guest speakers share their experiences and motivate students to achieve their goals . Notable alumni include Abdus Salam (Nobel laureate 1979 – Physics), Har Gobind Khorana (Nobel laureate 1968 – Medicine), and Asma Jahangir (Human rights activist and lawyer, co-founder of Human Rights Commission of Pakistan).
